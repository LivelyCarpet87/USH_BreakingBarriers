Thesis

An Apple II advertisement. Image Credit: The Verge
(Webster)

The Apple Company broke the barrier of technical complexity that prevented the general public from using computers and phones for everyday tasks. This is best manifested by the introduction of the Apple II computer and the iPhone 2G.

Computers before the Apple II required complex understanding and knowledge to use. The Apple II made computers more accessible to the general public by implementing a Graphical User Interface and pre-installed programs. This formed a new industry standard and broke down the barrier of complexity barring the computer from being used in everyday life.

Before Apple released the iPhone, phones were limited to only calling and texting due to their small screen and limited keyboard. Technical limitations made advanced features on old phones hard to use. The introduction of the iPhone made it possible to integrate more technologically advanced features into phones that could be used intuitively, again breaking the barrier of technical complexity barring phone users from fully exploring their phones' potential.
